DEPARTMENT OF MECHANICAL ENGINEERING — TECHNICAL EVENT RULES

GEAR UP

  • Assemble within one hour: Ensure the entire assembly process is completed within the allocated time frame of one hour.
  • Individual Participation: This event is meant for individual participants only. Each participant will work independently on their assembly task.
  • Safety Measures: All participants must wear safety shoes and adhere strictly to safety regulations throughout the assembly process.
  • Tool Usage: Use only the tools provided for the assembly task. Any unauthorized tools are strictly prohibited.
  • Compliance with Coordinator Instructions: Participants must follow all instructions provided by the event coordinators without any deviations.
  • Component Handling: Handle all components with utmost care to prevent any damage during the assembly process.
  • Evaluation Criteria: Participants will be evaluated based on three key criteria: accuracy, speed, and quality of the assembled product.

TECH STORM

  • Two-Round Structure: The event comprises two rounds, each serving a specific purpose.
  • First Round Presentation: In the initial round, participants will present their ideas within a time limit of 10 minutes. Slides may be used to support and enhance the presentation.
  • Second Round Challenge: The second round involves challenging other participants and demonstrating the superiority of your idea over others. Participants will engage in discussions, debates, or presentations to showcase the strengths of their concept.
  • Group Size Limit: Groups are limited to a maximum of four members.
  • Topic Announcement: The topic for the presentation will be provided 30 minutes before the event.

BRAIN BYTE BLITZ

  • Individual Event: The quiz competition is an individual event. Participants will compete on their own.
  • Question Categories: Questions will cover Basics of Mechanical Engineering and General Knowledge related to technology development.
  • Two Rounds: The competition consists of two rounds. The first round will be Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs), and the second round will feature questions requiring a single answer.
  • Answer Time Limit: Participants will have 30 seconds to answer each question.
  • Scoring: Each correctly answered question earns 1 point.
  • Mobile Phone Prohibition: Mobile phones are strictly prohibited during the competition to maintain fairness and integrity.
  • Tiebreaker: In the event of a tie, a tiebreaker will be provided to determine the final standings.

DRAFT MASTER

  • Individual Participation: The competition is open to individual participants only. Each participant will work independently on their design.
  • Single Round Event: The competition consists of a single round.
  • Design Task: Participants will be provided with a design task, and they are required to create a drawing using CAD software within a time limit of 3 hours.
  • Evaluation Criteria:
  • Timing: Adherence to the 3-hour time limit.
  • Neatness: Cleanliness and organization of the drawing.
  • Dimensional Perfection: Accuracy and precision of dimensions and measurements in the drawing.

DEPARTMENT OF CIVIL ENGINEERING — TECHNICAL EVENTS (RULES AND REGULATIONS)

1. BUILT XTRA (Bridge making using Ice-Cream Sticks)

  • Only 4 participants are allowed in a team.
  • A bridge is to be made with ice cream sticks; the materials required for making the bridge deck will be provided at the venue.
  • The minimum height of bridge should be 10 cm, maximum width of construction is 10 cm, and length (span) of the bridge is 50 cm.
  • Participants should complete the task within the stipulated time.
  • Duration of the competition is 2 hrs.
  • 1st and 2nd place holders will be awarded prize money and certificates.
  • Registration fee is 300/- per team.

2. ARKREATE (Arch Making Using Bricks)

  • Participants should form a team of maximum 4 members.
  • Arch should be constructed using 30 bricks.
  • Candidates should construct the arch by using 20 kg of sand.
  • Water, bricks, and sand will be provided.
  • Arch of maximum height 1 m and 90 cm span must be constructed.
  • Arch should be constructed within 30 minutes.
  • 1st and 2nd place holders will be awarded with prize money and certificates.
  • Registration fee is 300/- per team.

3. CLAY CRAFT CITY (Building township using clay)

  • Participants should form a team of maximum 4 members.
  • Participants will be provided with clay and water.
  • You can bring tools for sculpting, shaping and flattening the clay.
  • Additional items like tooth picks, ice cream sticks etc can be used for decorations.
  • Using of colours is not permitted.
  • Participants should complete the task within the stipulated time.
  • 1st and 2nd place holders will be awarded with prize money and certificates.
  • The duration of the competition is 2.5 hrs.
  • Registration fee is 300/- per team.

4. CADMANIA (Auto CAD Competition)

  • Maximum 1 participant per team.
  • Participants should draw a plan, elevation, and section of a building.
  • Points are noted for designing, drafting, detailing, labeling and coloring.
  • The final result will be judged based on completeness and optimum time.
  • 1st and 2nd place holders will be awarded with prize money and merit certificate.
  • Duration of the competition is 2 hrs.
  • Registration fee is 100/- per student.

5. OPEN SIM SIM (Treasure Hunt competition)

  • Participants should form a team of 4 members.
  • Hint consists of 6 clues out of which few may be misleading.
  • Few tasks are setup along with the treasure path, which must be performed to find the next clue.
  • Proximity of the hunt is the entire college premises.
  • College map will be provided.
  • The clues obtained must be kept safely till the end. Failing to do so may lead the team to lose the hunt.
  • Tampering or picking up more than one clue is strictly prohibited.
  • Miss-behaving with the volunteer and seeking help from outside the team may lead to team disqualification.
  • Organizers have the authority to disqualify any team if the rules are found to be violated.
  • Volunteer may be present at each stage of the event and will monitor your progress.
  • The team who gets all the clues and open the treasure box will be the winners.
  • First and second prize winners will be awarded cash prizes and certificates.
  • Registration fee is 200/- per team.

6. TOWER TAKEDOWN (Jenga)

  • Each team consists of one participant.
  • Stack the wooden blocks in a sturdy tower formation, three blocks per layer, alternating direction with each layer, but only from the middle layers (not at the top or bottom).
  • A timer is kept keeping track of each participant’s turn (60 sec).
  • Participants take turns, and only one hand should be used at a time to remove or place blocks.
  • Blocks should be only removed from the middle layers of the tower.
  • Participants must carefully remove blocks without causing the tower to collapse.
  • Once a block is touched, it must be removed, except for blocks that are visibly loose.
  • Participants can gently tap blocks to find a loose one, but they must commit to removing the first block they touch.
  • Each participant has one minute to complete their turn. If the time runs out before a move is made, the opposing participant gains a point.
  • The participant that causes the tower to collapse during their turn loses the game and the opposite participant will win.
  • In case of any disputes or discrepancies, a designated referee or judge will make the final decision.
  • First and second prize winners will be awarded cash prize and certificates.
  • Registration fee is 50/- student.

DEPARTMENT OF ELECTRONICS AND COMMUNICATION ENGINEERING — TECHNICAL EVENTS (RULES AND REGULATIONS)

1. CodeMend — Code Debugging Competition

  • Participants will be presented with a MATLAB code containing bugs and must rectify them within a 30-minute time frame.
  • The use of the internet, help windows, or inbuilt debugging tools is strictly prohibited. Failure to comply will result in disqualification.
  • The participant who successfully executes the program first within the designated time limit will be declared the winner.
  • In the event of a tie, the decision of the judging panel will be final.
  • Participants are expected to demonstrate respectful and sportsmanlike behaviour throughout the competition.
  • Comprehensive guidelines regarding the event will be furnished to the participants before the competition commences on the day of the event.

2. CirCure — Circuit Debugging Competition

Round 1: MCQs on Basic Circuit Concepts

  • Participants will answer multiple-choice questions (MCQs) on basic circuit concepts.
  • Each correct answer earns points; scores will be tallied at the end of the round.

Round 2: Fixing Mistakes in the Given Circuit Diagram

  • Participants will be provided with a circuit diagram containing errors/mistakes.
  • They must identify and correct as many mistakes as possible within the given time limit.
  • Participants with lowest completion time in Round 2 will advance. In case of tie, scores from Round 1 will be considered.

Round 3: Implementation of Corrected Circuit

  • Participants will implement the corrected circuit from Round 2 and produce the required output.
  • All materials needed for the circuit will be provided by the organizers.
  • Participants must complete the task within the given time frame.
  • Scoring will be based on the correctness of the output and the time taken to complete the task.

General Rules

  • Participants must adhere to instructions provided by event organizers.
  • Any form of misconduct or cheating will result in immediate disqualification.
  • Decisions made by judges or event organizers are final.
  • Participants cannot bring their own materials or components unless specified.
  • Equipment and tools must be handled with care.
  • Professional and respectful conduct is expected throughout the competition.

3. HydroSprint

  • Each participant must complete the track in shortest possible time.
  • The minimum time taken to finish the race will declare the winner.
  • The track will contain obstacle that must be successfully crossed.

4. Hobbycuit — Hobby Circuit Competition

  • Task Allocation: Participants will be given 5 circuit tasks to complete within the competition.
  • Component Usage: Only the provided components can be used for circuit building.
  • Time Limit: The total time span for completing the tasks is 1 hour.
  • Scoring Criteria: Participants will be awarded based on the maximum number of successfully executed tasks.
  • Tie-Breaker Rule: In case of a tie, 2 additional tasks will be given, and the participant who executes them first will be declared the winner.
  • Safety & Fair Play: All participants must follow safety guidelines and maintain fair competition practices.

5. OhmRaid — Circuit Treasure Hunt

General Rules

  • Team Composition: Each team can have upto 4 members.
  • Eligibility: Participants can be from any branch.
  • Time Limit: The entire treasure hunt will have a fixed duration of 3 hours.
  • Integrity: Any form of cheating, tampering with clues, or external help is strictly prohibited.
  • Fair Play: Teams must respect other participants and not interfere with their progress.
  • Hunt Boundaries: The treasure hunt will take place within Main Building and Electronics Circuits laboratory.

Game Rules

  • Clue Structure: Each clue will be based on electronics, circuits, or communication concepts and will lead to the next location.
  • Solving Clues: Teams must decode technical hints, solve puzzles, or perform mini-tasks to proceed.
  • Use of Mobile Phones: Unless specified, the use of mobile phones, calculators, or the internet is prohibited.
  • Hint System: Teams may receive a limited number of hints with a penalty of additional time.
  • Checkpoint Verification: Teams must report to designated checkpoints before proceeding to the next stage.

Winning Criteria

  • Completion Order: The team that finds the final treasure first will be declared the winner.
  • Bonus Points: Extra points may be awarded for solving clues quickly or additional technical challenges.
  • Disqualification: Any violation of the rules, misconduct, or bypassing clues will result in immediate disqualification.

DEPARTMENT OF APPLIED SCIENCE AND HUMANITIES — TECHNICAL EVENTS (RULES AND REGULATIONS)

1. QUADRA (ROBO RACE)

A thrilling remote-controlled car race set on a challenging off-road track, where speed and stability determine the ultimate winner.

Game Rules

  • The robot that completes the track in the shortest time with maximum stability will be declared the winner.
  • Remote-controlled cars will be provided by the organizers.
  • The competition will consist of a single round (no semifinals or finals).
  • A maximum of four participants from each year are allowed to participate in the competition.
  • Participants must ensure that the robot does not damage the arena.
  • No practice runs will be allowed on the track before the event.
  • If a robot starts before the official start signal, the run will be restarted and the team will be given one more chance. Repeating this will result in disqualification.
  • Evaluation will be based on: (a) time taken to complete the track, (b) number of checkpoints successfully cleared.
  • No second attempt will be given once the track is completed, even if the score is low.
  • Any form of unethical behavior will lead to disqualification.
  • Faculty coordinators reserve the right to make the final decision on any matter during the event.
  • The judge’s decision will be final and binding.

2. ROBO CUP (ROBO SOCCER)

An exciting football knockout competition played using wired robots, where teams compete to score goals and outplay their opponents.

Bot Specifications

  • Two bots of equal dimensions will be provided to the participants.

Arena Specifications

  • Arena size: 8 × 4 feet.
  • The arena will have walled boundaries.
  • A plastic ball will be used for the game.

Rules and Regulations

  • Each team can have a maximum of 2 members.
  • A maximum of two teams from each year is allowed.
  • The match will begin with a toss: the winning team gets initial possession at the center; the opposing team will defend their goal post.
  • The match will consist of two halves.
  • Match duration: initial knockout matches: 3 minutes per half; quarterfinals & semifinals: 3 minutes per half; final: 5 minutes per half.
  • In case of a draw, 2 minutes of extra time will be given, followed by a shootout if required.
  • Teams will switch sides after halftime.
  • The match will be paused if wires of the bots get entangled.
  • Bots must not damage the opponent’s bot.
  • If one team is disqualified, the opposing team will win by default.
  • Only two team members are allowed inside the arena during the match: one member will control the bot; the other member will manage the wire.
  • The structure of the robot must not be modified during the competition.
  • Any form of misconduct or rule violation may lead to disqualification.
  • The decisions of the referees and event coordinators will be final and binding in all cases.

3. PEDAL PURSUIT (CYCLE CHALLENGE)

A fun and challenging event where participants ride a “Reverse Brain Cycle,” testing their balance, coordination, and control.

Rules

  • Each contestant must ride the cycle for a distance of 50 meters.
  • A maximum of four participants from each year are allowed to participate in the competition.
  • Each participant will be given two chances.
  • The cycle must not be pushed using the feet. Doing so will be considered a foul and will lead to disqualification.
  • Evaluation will be based on (in order of priority): (a) time taken to complete the ride, (b) number of checkpoints successfully cleared.
  • Any form of misbehaviour or misconduct may result in disqualification.
  • Faculty coordinators reserve the right to make the final decision on any matter during the event.
  • The judge’s decision will be final and binding.

4. BUZZ BLITZ (BUZZER WIRE GAME)

A steady-hand challenge where participants must carefully navigate a loop along a twisted wire without making contact.

Game Rules

  • Participants must stand at the designated starting point.
  • A maximum of four participants from each year are allowed to participate in the competition.
  • The timer will start as soon as the participant begins the game.
  • The objective is to move the metal loop from the start to the finish along the wire without touching it.
  • If the loop touches the wire at any point, the buzzer will sound, and the attempt will immediately end.
  • A participant’s turn ends when they successfully reach the end of the wire, the buzzer sounds due to contact, or the 2-minute time limit is reached.
  • The time taken to complete the task will be recorded.
  • Each participant will be given two attempts, and the best time (within the time limit) will be considered.
  • Scoring is based on completion time — the faster the time, the better the score.
  • The participant with the fastest successful time within the time limit will be declared the winner.
  • The judge’s decision will be final and binding.

5. WireX (Wiring Challenge)

An electrical wiring competition where participants demonstrate their technical skills by completing a circuit based on a given diagram, focusing on accuracy, safety, and neat execution.

Rules and Regulations

  • Team Formation: Each team can have a maximum of 2 participants. Participants must register before the competition begins. A maximum of two teams from each year is allowed.
  • Time Limit: The total time allotted for completing the wiring task is 30 minutes.
  • Materials and Tools: Basic tools (screwdriver, tester, pliers, wire stripper, etc.) will be provided. Participants must use only the materials and tools provided by the organizers.
  • Task: Teams will be given a wiring diagram or circuit layout. The wiring must be completed exactly as per the given diagram.
  • Safety Rules: Proper insulation and safe wiring practices must be strictly followed. Power supply must not be connected until approval is given by the judge. All electrical safety instructions must be followed at all times.
  • Judging Criteria: Accuracy of wiring; neatness and cable management; time taken to complete the task; adherence to safety practices; proper functioning of the circuit.
  • Penalties: Incorrect connections or unsafe wiring will result in penalty marks. Damaging equipment may lead to disqualification.
  • Result: The team with the highest overall score based on the judging criteria will be declared the winner.
  • Judge’s Decision: The decision of the judges will be final and binding.

6. TRAP BREAKERS

An exciting challenge where participants control a car along a predefined path while avoiding dynamic obstacles like chasing lights and automatic gates, testing precision, timing, and control.

Rules

  • The car must start from the designated starting point when the timer begins.
  • Participants must follow the marked path to reach the parking area.
  • Reverse movement of the car is strictly not allowed at any point during the game.
  • A moving light will act as a “catcher” that chases the car throughout the course.
  • The light must not catch or overtake the car. If it does, the participant will be disqualified.
  • The car must cross the level crossing gate within the allotted time.
  • If the car fails to cross before the time limit, the gate will automatically close, resulting in the end of the attempt.
  • The car must not collide with the level crossing gate while it is closing or when it is closed.

1. TEXPLORE (QUIZ)

  • Only team entries are allowed and each team should not have more than two members. Replacement of any participant of a team is not allowed after registration.
  • Participants are not permitted to use mobile phones or other electronic gadgets during the quiz.
  • Questions will be in multiple formats, including multiple-choice, true/false statements, one-word answers, and visual-based questions.
  • The quizmaster's decision will be final and cannot be challenged.
  • In case more than 10 teams register, a Qualifying Round will be conducted where each team will receive a question paper containing 20 objective-type questions.
  • The time limit for this round is 20 minutes (1 minute for each question), with 1 mark awarded per correct answer. The top 10 teams with the highest scores will qualify for the stage rounds, and the venue and time will be announced during the qualifying round.

The Stage Rounds (Three rounds)

  • General Round: Each team will be asked three objective questions with options, with 10 marks for a correct answer and a 5-mark deduction for a wrong answer. Teams will have 15 seconds to answer. If a team cannot answer, it can be passed and the first team to hoist the pluck card will earn 5 marks for a correct answer and lose 2 marks for a wrong one. Discussion within the team is allowed. The top 6 teams advance to the Visual Round.
  • Visual Round: Each team will answer questions related to an image/logo displayed on the screen. This round consists of three rounds, with each team receiving one image per round. Correct answers earn 10 marks, while wrong answers incur a 5-mark deduction. Answering time is 10 seconds, and no passing is allowed to the next team (Audience can answer subjected to Quiz master’s request.). The top 4 teams proceed to the Rapid-Fire Round.
  • Rapid Fire Round: Each team must answer 10 questions within 90 seconds. One member answers but can take help from their partner. If a team cannot answer the question, they can pass for the next question till they finish all ten questions. 10 marks are awarded for each correct answer, and 5 marks are deducted for each wrong answer.
  • The team with the highest score at the end will be declared the winner.
  • Participants can earn up to 40 activity points based on their performance.
  • No Registration fee for Internal Participants.
  • Registration fee is ₹150/- per team (External participants).

3. BLIND CODING

Round 2 (Final Round)
